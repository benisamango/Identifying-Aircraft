Identifying Aircraft Based on Engines

Aircraft can often be identified by their engine types. Different aircraft models use distinct engine configurations and designs. For example:

  • Jet engines: Common on commercial airliners like Boeing 737 or Airbus A320.
  • Turboprop engines: Found on regional aircraft such as Bombardier Q400 or ATR 72.
  • Turbofan engines: Used on larger jets like Boeing 787 or Airbus A350.
  • Piston engines: Typically seen on smaller propeller-driven aircraft.
